# Maximo Maintenance Cost

> **FIRST:** load the `maximo-overview` skill for the universal Maximo data model
> and the universal gotchas (SITEID composite keys, status-is-a-synonym-domain,
> HISTORYFLAG, app-server-timezone datetimes, ISTASK, WOCLASS). This skill APPLIES
> those patterns and adds the cost-specific depth on top.

Help maintenance managers, finance controllers, and reliability engineers query
and analyze maintenance costs across labor, materials, contractors, and tools.

## When to use

- "Maintenance cost per asset last year"
- "Top assets by maintenance cost"
- "Cost variance — estimate vs actual"
- "Budget vs actual spend by site"
- "PM cost vs CM cost ratio"
- "Contractor spend by vendor"
- "Labor cost vs material cost split", "overtime cost by craft"
- "Cost per operating hour for critical assets"
- "Cost trend over time"
- "Cost of unplanned downtime / cost of failure"
- "Bad-actor assets by cost"

For operational backlog / WO-count analytics (not cost-focused), use
`maximo-work-orders`. For inventory carrying cost / item-level costing
methodology, use `maximo-inventory`. For failure-rate / MTBF / reactive-vs-
proactive ratios, use `maximo-reliability`.

## Top gotchas

1. **Cost columns are per-record — they do NOT auto-roll-up to the parent.**
   `ACTLABCOST`/`ACTMATCOST` post to the record where the labor/material was
   incurred; they do NOT roll up to the parent WO automatically. `ACTTOTALCOST`
   is NON-PERSISTENT (computed) and may be absent in silver — never depend on it.
   For a WO-tree total, recurse the hierarchy or aggregate `LABTRANS`/
   `MATUSETRANS` across all WOs in the tree. See [gotchas.md](gotchas.md) #2.
2. **Follow-up costs do NOT roll up to the originator.** A WO created from another
   record is a `Follow-up` and lives in a SEPARATE hierarchy — its cost/labor are
   NOT under the originator and are NOT reachable via `PARENT`. Trace via
   `ORIGRECORDID`/`ORIGRECORDCLASS` and the `RELATEDRECORD` table. See
   [gotchas.md](gotchas.md) #11.
3. **Multi-currency aggregation = wrong number.** `WORKORDER.WOCURRENCY` /
   `EXCHANGERATE` vary across orgs/sites; summing `LINECOST` across currencies is
   meaningless. Normalize to one reporting currency, or group by currency. Shipped
   UDFs assume single-currency. See [gotchas.md](gotchas.md) #3.
4. **Don't trust `WORKTYPE='PM'` as "generated by a PM".** `PMNUM IS NOT NULL` =
   PM-generated (source); `WORKTYPE` = business intent. They diverge. Cross-check;
   confirm the customer's convention. See [gotchas.md](gotchas.md) #8.
5. **`WORKORDER.ACTLABCOST` ≠ `SUM(LABTRANS.LINECOST)` in practice.** They should
   match but often don't (post-close Edit-History appends land in `LABTRANS`,
   header may not re-settle). Prefer `LABTRANS`/`MATUSETRANS` for granular and
   period-attributed analytics; reconcile and disclose discrepancies. See
   [gotchas.md](gotchas.md) #1.

Applying overview mechanics in cost SQL (one line each, full detail in overview):
resolve `COMP`/`CLOSE` via `SYNONYMDOMAIN` (status is a synonym domain — overview
gotcha F2); include closed WOs explicitly because `HISTORYFLAG=1` history records
drop out of the IBM-shipped List views and most completed-cost lives there
(overview F3); `COMP`≠`CLOSE` so key "completed cost" on `COMP`-or-later (overview
F8); datetimes are app-server-timezone, so be careful bucketing spend by day/week/
month across sites (overview F4); always join on `SITEID` (overview F1) and filter
`WOCLASS='WORKORDER'` for normal WO cost (overview F9).

## Questions to surface first

Ask these before producing a cost number — each has no defensible default:

1. **Currency** — "Are your sites multi-currency (does `WORKORDER.WOCURRENCY`
   vary)? If so, what single reporting currency should I normalize to?" Summing
   line costs across currencies is meaningless.
2. **Cost rollup convention** — "For a parent WO with child tasks/child WOs, do
   you want the parent header cost only, or the rolled-up cost of the whole tree?"
   Maximo does NOT auto-roll-up; the convention is customer-specific.
3. **PM-vs-CM definition** — "Do you classify preventive vs corrective by
   `PMNUM IS NOT NULL` (PM-generated source) or by `WORKTYPE` codes (business
   intent)? They are different and diverge in your data."
4. **Period attribution** — "Should spend be attributed by transaction date
   (`LABTRANS`/`MATUSETRANS` date — true period consumption) or by WO close date
   (`ACTFINISH`)? Long-running WOs make these disagree."
5. **Completed-cost cutoff** — "Does 'completed cost' mean `COMP`-or-later, or
   only `CLOSE`d WOs? Many shops never `CLOSE`, so a `CLOSE`-only filter
   undercounts."
6. **Header vs transaction source** — "For the cost of record, use the
   `WORKORDER` header (`ACTLABCOST`/`ACTMATCOST`) or the transaction tables
   (`LABTRANS`/`MATUSETRANS`)? Prefer transactions for granularity; header is the
   audit-of-record on closed WOs."

## Pre-flight (per session)

1. **Silver/gold catalog + schema** — confirm via workspace glossary; cache.
2. **App-server timezone** — confirm the deployment's datetime timezone (a
   `maximo-setup` interview fact) before bucketing spend across sites.
3. **Status synonyms** — confirm whether the customer renamed `WOSTATUS` synonyms
   (resolve via `SYNONYMDOMAIN`, per overview); cache the resolved set for
   `COMP`/`CLOSE`.
4. **Contractor convention** — confirm how this customer marks contractor labor
   (`LABOR.VENDOR`, `LABOR.LABORTYPE`, or a synthetic laborcode); cache.

## What NOT to do

- **Don't sum `LINECOST` across currencies** — `WORKORDER.WOCURRENCY` may vary by
  site (ledger F12, owned here).
- **Don't assume parent WOs roll up child costs automatically** — they don't, and
  `ACTTOTALCOST` is non-persistent (ledger F6, owned here). See gotcha 2.
- **Don't assume follow-up WO costs roll up to the originator** — separate
  hierarchy; trace via `RELATEDRECORD`/`ORIGRECORDID`, not `PARENT` (ledger F7).
- **Don't filter PM-generated WOs by `WORKTYPE = 'PM'` alone** — use
  `PMNUM IS NOT NULL` for "generated by a PM"; `WORKTYPE` for business intent.
- **Don't include WOs without an asset** in asset-attributed cost reports — they
  materially distort cost-per-asset.
- **Don't conflate `WORKORDER.ACTLABCOST` with `SUM(LABTRANS.LINECOST)`** —
  reconcile; prefer LABTRANS for granular/period analysis.
- **Don't re-teach universal mechanics** (SITEID, status-synonym, HISTORYFLAG,
  WOCLASS, ISTASK, timezone) — APPLY them and point to `maximo-overview`.
- **Don't author GL/accounting-period or PO/invoice cost** — out of scope (see
  below); PO/invoice actuals are `maximo-procurement`'s charter.

## Composes with

- `maximo-overview` — universal model + all cross-cutting mechanics (apply, don't
  re-teach).
- `maximo-work-orders` — WO header cost columns, `WPLABOR`/`WPMATERIAL` estimate,
  ISTASK tasks-vs-child-WO semantics for tree rollups. **DEFER** WO lifecycle/
  status mechanics there.
- `maximo-reliability` — failure events for cost-of-failure (events from
  reliability × cost from here). **DEFER** MTBF/failure-rate/reactive-vs-proactive
  there; reactive-vs-proactive and planned-vs-unplanned ratios are computed on
  LABOR HOURS, not cost — that is reliability's charter.
- `maximo-inventory` — item costing method (`INVENTORY.COSTMETHOD`, avg/FIFO/LIFO/
  standard) drives `MATUSETRANS.LINECOST`. **DEFER** carrying cost / reorder /
  issue mechanics there.
- `maximo-labor-resources` — `LABOR`/`CRAFT` master, contractor identification.
  **DEFER** capacity/qualifications/crew there.
- `maximo-asset-hierarchy` — `LOCANCESTOR` closure table for location rollups
  (`v_location_cost_summary`). **DEFER** hierarchy traversal mechanics there.
- `maximo-pm-planning` — PM-vs-CM cost ratio consumes this skill's UDF.
- `maximo-procurement` — what was *paid to a vendor* (PO/invoice actuals, vendor spend, price variance). **DEFER** PO/invoice/vendor cost there; this skill is WO-side consumed cost (avg/standard), not procurement spend.

## What's intentionally out of scope

- **GL account integration** (`GLACCOUNT`, charge-account joins to the customer
  chart-of-accounts). Very customer-specific — build a workspace-level skill if
  needed.
- **Accounting-period / interface-table transactions** — Maximo's accounting
  integration is via interface tables; out of charter for analytics-side skills.
- **PO / invoice cost** — that's `maximo-procurement`. This skill stops at WO-side actuals
  (what was consumed at avg/standard cost), not what was paid to a vendor.
